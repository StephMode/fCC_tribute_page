Adalbert Stifter

The Biedermeier author of the Prose of "Quiet Storm"

Here's a brief time line of Adalbert Stifter's life

  • 1805 Born in Oberplan in Bohemia (now Horní Planá in the Czech Republic).
  • 1817 Father, Johann Stifter, died in an accident.
  • 1826 Began studying law at the University of Vienna.
  • 1828 Fell in love with Fanny Greipl; relationship ended in 1833 due to her parents' disapproval.
  • 1835 Became engaged to Amalia Mohaupt.
  • 1837 Married Amalia Mohaupt; marriage was unhappy.
  • 1840 Published first story, "Der Condor," marking the start of his writing career.
  • 1848 Supported revolutions, visited Linz, later moved there in 1849.
  • 1850 Appointed supervisor of elementary schools for Upper Austria.
  • 1863 Health began to decline.
  • 1867 Became seriously ill with cirrhosis of the liver.
  • 1868 Attempted suicide on 25 January; died three days later.

"Stifter is just one of the greatest prose writers of the German language."

- Stephan Model
